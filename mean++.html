K-Means++ Clustering

K-Means ++ Clustering is similar to K-Means, with one exception. Choosing the initial centroid points is done via a weighting process, instead of a randomization one, so that the initial centroids are more likely to be spread out. Given that data is present, the steps to the algorithm are completed as follows. Note how steps 2-4 are the same as the k-means algorithm.

  1. Randomly choose/initialize k centroids
    1. Randomly choose/initialize 1 centroids
    2. Calculate the distance from each point to its nearest centroid
    3. Use a weighted probability distribution where a point is chosen to be the center of a new centroid with probability proportional to its step B distance squared
    4. Repeat steps B-C until k centroids are chosen
  2. Assign each point in the dataset to its nearest centroid
  3. Calculate the new mean of each centroid, using the points that were classified into its group
  4. Repeat steps 2-3 until the classified values in each centroid remain unchanged
